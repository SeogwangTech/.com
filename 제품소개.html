Test Name Chelating Test
Test Date 2012.03.28
Test Location se gwang R&D Center
Objective Evaluate the chelating ability of CM-800 against various metal ions (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺) to prevent precipitation and agglutination.
Test Method - Prepare separate solutions of CaCl₂ (0.41g/L, 1500ppm), MgCl₂·6H₂O (0.36g/L, 1000ppm), FeCl₂·4H₂O (0.14g/L, 1000ppm)
- Add 1g/L of MONO CM-800 into each solution
- Add 1mL of NaOH (10%) to each beaker
- Heat each solution at boiling point for 10 minutes
- Let cool and observe agglutination and clarity
- Determine performance by reduced precipitate generation and increased solution transparency
Evaluation Criteria - Clarity: Indicates successful chelation
- Less precipitate: Indicates better dispersion & metal ion blocking performance
- Optimal pH environment: Maintains stability of chelating agent
Visual Observations Beakers labeled Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺, Fe³⁺ displayed notable clarity and minimal agglutination post-treatment with MONO CM-800.
